Monty Python's Life of Brian

Monty Python's Life of Brian, also known as Life of Brian, is a 1979 British religious satire comedy film starring and written by the comedy group Monty Python (Graham Chapman, John Cleese, Terry Gilliam, Eric Idle, Terry Jones and Michael Palin)

Life of Brian has regularly been cited as a significant contender for the title "greatest comedy film of all time"
and has been named as such in polls conducted by Total Film magazine in 2000,[41] the British TV network Channel 4 in 2006[42] and The Guardian newspaper in 2007.[43] Rotten Tomatoes lists it as one of the best reviewed
with a 96% approval rating from 44 published reviews. A 2011 poll by Time Out magazine ranked it as the third greatest comedy film ever made,[44] behind Airplane! and This is Spinal Tap.
